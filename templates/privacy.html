Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

1. Information We Collect

When you create a Spendly account, we collect basic information such as your name, email address, and a securely hashed password. As you use the Service, we also collect the expense data you choose to enter, including amounts, categories, dates, and descriptions.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information solely to provide and improve the Service: to authenticate your account, store and display your expenses, generate summaries and category breakdowns, and communicate with you about your account when necessary. We do not use your financial data for advertising or profiling.

3. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored on secured servers and protected using industry standard practices. Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text. While we take reasonable measures to protect your information, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

4. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share limited data with service providers (such as hosting and infrastructure vendors) strictly to operate the Service, or when required by law. Aggregated, anonymized usage statistics that cannot identify you may be used to improve the Service.

5. Your Rights and Choices

You can view, edit, or delete your expense entries at any time from within the Service. You may also request deletion of your account and all associated data by contacting us. Once deleted, your data cannot be recovered.

6. Cookies and Tracking

Spendly uses essential cookies to keep you signed in and to remember your session. We do not use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the Service after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
